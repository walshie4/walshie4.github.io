Awesome Sites

  • Forcast.io
    • An awesome weather site which takes input from many other weather sites and gives some of the most accurate weather predictions I've ever seen
  • Hacker News
    • If you're tech minded, just give it a few vists and you'll be hooked. Always loads of great content.
  • TorProject
    • Home to some of the most important projects to those who care about anonimity on the internet.
  • ZenHabits
    • Great website full of helpful advice for almost everything
  • Antrainik
    • The home to the bodyweight fitness workout routine I build my workouts off of.
  • Kevin Informatics - Graph TV
    • Very cool website which will allow you to see how the IMDb ratings of different shows has changed throughout their production. Just type in a show name and manipulate the output in your style of viewing.
  • Cyrpto.cat
    • Encrypted chat client, add Tor for an even more secure system.
  • Lamassu
    • A company devoted to making Bitcoin ATM's a reality
  • Turtl
    • A Dropbox alternative that puts your security first. I was thrilled to find this and even more thrilled when I read the below quote from a blog post of theirs.

    Using this system, Turtl effectively knows nothing about what you’re storing at all. Not only does Turtl have zero knowledge of your data, the same applies for hackers and government agencies as well.

    Only you and those you share with are able to view any of your data.

    And…

    We don’t know what you’re storing. Neither do copyright holders. Neither does the government.

    That’s how we like it =]

    And that's how I like it too. :]

    And after all that, just when I thought it couldn't get any better I found out it's open-source. :D

  • PyCrypto intro
    • A very handy and informative introduction to some basic concepts in cryptography, and in particular the Python module PyCrypto. Written by Kyle Isom.
  • XKCD
    • Awesome comic website that has some really cool and intresting, as well as sometimes funny comics.
  • RegExr
    • An awesome interactive way to become more familiar with using regular expressions.
  • TinyBuddha
    • A website of quotes, blog posts, and other things full of 'simple wisdom for complex lives.'
  • StartPage
    • As per their tagline it is 'the world's most private search engine' and a quick look at their privacy page will confirm this.
  • Prism-Break
    • Break free from unwanted survillence with open source, security minded replacements for proprietary software.
