The Privacy Debate

As I hope many of you are aware there recently has been a growing debate about our privacy when using electronic / modern telecommunications technology. A lot of this debate surrounds Edward Snowden and the NSA, however I do not want to attempt to force feed you my point of view. I agree with the words of NSA Deputy Director Richard Ledgett when he said, (in this TED talk regarding NSA surviellence programs) that the best thing we can all do is to, "learn the facts."

Some TED talks

  • As linked above, this talk actually features Richard Ledgett live answering questions at a TED conference.

  • This is a talk given in Brussels in which Cybersecurity Expert Mikko Hypponen brings up some excellent points about what these actions (primarily those made by the NSA) mean for us as a modern population.

  • This talk features Edward Snowden himself live via robot and webcam, and offers insight into his intentions with the leak, as well as his take on some of the programs he uncovered. Also, some talk as to what he believes are the next steps.

Words from others on this subject

Awesome Open-source Projects

  • OpenSpritz
    • If you don't know about Spritz you should check it out. It's incredible. But this project isn't Spritz, it's just a nice reminder of why I love the open-source community. OpenSpritz is an open-source rendition of the Spritz software written in Javascript. For more information read the blog post by the original author, or check out the Github project page.
  • Popcorn Time
    • Popcorn time is another just incredible project. It allows you to stream movies using the power of BitTorrent, and it is just soooooo cool. I hope to see forks (if not help out on those forks) for watching TV shows, or listening to music.
  • iOS Onion Browser
    • I love TOR and of course I love open-source so when I found out that the Onion Browser for iOS was open source I was thrilled. Putting the power of TOR onto iOS (not even just jailbroken iOS) is something I believe is almost necessary, not to mention really cool. You can check out the offical site here.
